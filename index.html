About Rishikesh

A River City With Many Layers

Rishikesh, nestled at the foothills of the Garhwal Himalayas in Uttarakhand, is one of India's most compelling travel destinations. Known for the sacred flow of the Ganga, it balances deep spiritual heritage with a thriving adventure scene. Ancient temples, evening aarti ceremonies, yoga schools, forest trails, and suspension bridges together shape a city that feels both timeless and alive. Whether you are here for inner calm, cultural immersion, or outdoor thrills, Rishikesh offers layered experiences for every kind of traveler.

Travel Snapshot

  • Region: Garhwal, Uttarakhand
  • Altitude: Approx. 372 m
  • Best for: Yoga and Adventure
  • Ideal stay: 3 to 5 days

Top Attractions

Landmarks You Should Not Miss

From sacred ghats to iconic bridges, these places capture the visual and cultural identity of Rishikesh.

Triveni Ghat

A sacred riverfront where evening Ganga Aarti transforms the atmosphere into a spiritual spectacle.

Gita Bhawan

A revered spiritual complex with satsangs, murals, and serene river-facing spaces.

Laxman Jhula

An iconic suspension bridge with panoramic views of temples, ghats, and mountain horizons.

Ram Jhula

A lively crossing connecting ashrams, cafes, and local markets across the Ganges.

Parmarth Niketan

One of the largest ashrams in the region, known for yoga sessions and peaceful surroundings.

Beatles Ashram

Graffiti-clad meditation domes and tranquil trails preserve the city's famous musical legacy.
